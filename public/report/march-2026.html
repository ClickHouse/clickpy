PyPI Usage Report — March 2026

Python package download analytics • Powered by ClickPy • Published April 6, 2026

Overview

March 2026 was a record month for PyPI by raw download volume. The ecosystem logged 136.7 billion downloads across 31 days, up from 108.0 billion in February. On a day-adjusted basis, average daily downloads grew from 3.86 billion to 4.41 billion (+14.3%), suggesting genuine demand acceleration beyond the calendar difference. The AI-tooling wave that began accelerating in late 2025 continued unabated, with several inference, agent-framework, and RAG-related packages posting triple- and quadruple-digit month-over-month growth rates.

Geographic Distribution

The United States remained the dominant source of PyPI traffic with 101.0 billion downloads, accounting for roughly 74% of all global activity. This concentration reflects the density of US-based cloud infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and automated build systems that produce the majority of bot-driven installs. Month-over-month, US volume grew +27.6%.

Europe's top hubs — Ireland (4.7B), Germany (4.4B), the Netherlands (3.1B), and the United Kingdom (3.0B) — held their positions. Ireland's outsized share relative to population reflects the country's role as a cloud hosting hub for several major providers. France (+17.1%) and Sweden (+21.3%) posted healthy double-digit gains.

Notable regional surges: Finland (+132.4%), Hong Kong (+93.9%), and China (+91.2%) all roughly doubled their download volumes compared to February. Finland and Hong Kong's jumps likely reflect infrastructure scaling or new CI fleet deployments. China's continued growth signals increasing Python adoption for AI/ML workloads in that market. In contrast, Brazil posted the only notable decline in the top 20 at −19.7%, possibly due to a shift in CI infrastructure or seasonal factors.

Most Popular Packages

The top of the chart remains firmly in the hands of foundational infrastructure packages. boto3 led all packages with 1.88 billion downloads, reinforcing the AWS SDK's indispensable role in Python CI pipelines. Core utilities packaging, setuptools, urllib3, and certifi followed in tight formation between 1.3B and 1.6B, consistent with their role as transitive dependencies pulled in by virtually every Python project.

numpy held at 871 million downloads, continuing its position as the foundation for numerical Python. pydantic reached 762 million, with downstream growth from its AI ecosystem further lifting the base package's numbers. six remains a top-15 fixture at 934 million — a testament to how deeply Python 2/3 compatibility shims are embedded in the dependency graph even years after Python 2's EOL.

Emerging Packages

Packages below had at least 1 million downloads in both February and March 2026 and posted the sharpest month-over-month growth. This threshold filters out noise from packages starting from a near-zero base while still surfacing genuinely accelerating adoption.

March's emerging package landscape is almost entirely shaped by one mega-trend: AI agent infrastructure. The packages gaining the most traction are either LLM inference runtimes, agent frameworks, RAG data-connectors, or developer observability tools built around those stacks. Below are the standout movers.

arxiv +1,453% AI

From 2.7 million downloads in February to 42.4 million in March — the arxiv Python client saw the steepest growth of any qualifying package this month. This library provides programmatic access to the arXiv preprint repository. Its explosive adoption almost certainly reflects its use in AI agent pipelines that retrieve research papers on demand, either as tools for code-generation agents or for RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) knowledge bases. The timing aligns with numerous open-source "research agent" projects that gained traction on GitHub in early 2026.

sglang +1,131% AI

SGLang is a structured generation language and high-performance LLM inference runtime developed by researchers at UC Berkeley and LMSYS. Jumping from 2.9 million to 36.0 million downloads, it is clearly becoming a go-to backend for teams self-hosting open-weight models. SGLang's throughput advantages over naive vLLM setups and its constrained-decoding support for structured outputs make it attractive for production agent deployments.

zarr +958% AI

Zarr is a chunked, compressed, N-dimensional array format widely used in scientific computing and increasingly in AI/ML pipelines for storing large embedding matrices and model checkpoints. Growing from 4.7 million to 49.6 million downloads, it appears to have gained traction as a standard for sharing multimodal datasets and model artifacts. Its cloud-native design (works directly on S3, GCS, Azure Blob) makes it a natural fit for distributed training and serving workflows.

pydantic-ai-slim +584% AI & pydantic-graph +533%

These two packages from the Pydantic team are growing in tandem and form the core of the Pydantic AI agent framework. pydantic-ai-slim went from 15.6 million to 106.9 million downloads; pydantic-graph from 13.6 million to 86.2 million. Their growth reflects both organic adoption of the framework and its integration as a dependency in other popular tooling. The slim variant's slightly higher volume suggests many users are opting for the lighter install without bundled model providers. Companion package logfire (+251%, 20.9M downloads) — Pydantic's observability SDK — also surged, confirming that teams are running these agents in production and need structured tracing.

wikipedia-api +506% AI

Growing from 6.5 million to 39.4 million downloads, wikipedia-api is another clear beneficiary of the RAG and AI-agent boom. Wikipedia remains one of the most commonly used corpora for grounding LLM responses, and this library provides a clean Python interface to the MediaWiki API. Its growth mirrors that of arxiv and suggests that "knowledge retrieval" tooling is now a mainstream component of AI application stacks.

fastapi-mcp +233% AI

fastapi-mcp automatically exposes FastAPI routes as MCP (Model Context Protocol) tools, enabling any FastAPI service to be consumed directly by LLM agents. Growing from 12.5 million to 41.6 million downloads, this package is a strong signal that the MCP standard — originally popularised by Anthropic — is seeing rapid enterprise adoption. Teams are retrofitting existing APIs rather than rewriting them, and fastapi-mcp makes that essentially a one-liner.

pymupdf4llm +257% AI

A PDF-to-Markdown converter specifically designed for LLM ingestion pipelines, growing from 11.0 million to 39.2 million downloads. As enterprises build document-understanding agents over their internal knowledge bases (contracts, reports, manuals), robust PDF-to-text extraction has become a critical preprocessing step. pymupdf4llm handles complex layouts, tables, and mixed-language documents better than generic tools, which explains its accelerating uptake.

requests-cache +210% Infra

Growing from 7.5 million to 23.3 million downloads, requests-cache adds transparent HTTP response caching to the requests library. Its growth is likely a secondary effect of the AI tooling surge: agent pipelines that repeatedly retrieve the same external data (APIs, knowledge bases, web pages) benefit greatly from caching, reducing latency and API costs. It is also commonly pulled in by CI systems to speed up test runs.

Key Takeaways

March 2026 confirms that the Python ecosystem is in an AI-infrastructure buildout phase. The packages gaining momentum are not experimental curiosities — they are reaching tens of millions of monthly downloads, which puts them in the same tier as established DevOps and data-engineering tools. The Pydantic AI ecosystem (ai-slim, pydantic-graph, logfire) is consolidating as a platform stack, not just a collection of loosely related packages. MCP adoption via fastapi-mcp is accelerating faster than most protocol-layer libraries typically do at this stage.

On the geographic front, watch the Asia-Pacific region closely. Singapore, Hong Kong, Australia, and China all posted above-average growth, collectively adding over 2 billion additional downloads compared to February. If this trend continues, APAC's combined share could rival Western Europe's within the next few months.

RubyGems Downloads — March 2026

Published April 6, 2026  ·  Data source: rubygems.org via ClickHouse  ·  Covering 2026-03-01 → 2026-03-31

Overview

March 2026 was a strong month for the RubyGems ecosystem. The United States continued its dominant position as the single largest download source, and overall global volume surged compared to February — driven partly by a longer calendar month and partly by real growth in several emerging markets. A handful of gems saw explosive month-over-month increases, headlined by the mcp gem whose adoption more than ninefold in a single month as AI tooling integration with Ruby apps accelerates.

Notable Shifts

United States (+20.1%) remains the clear leader, accounting for roughly 75% of all tracked downloads. The growth largely reflects more CI/CD pipeline activity and cloud-based Ruby workloads running on AWS and GCP infrastructure in US regions.

Ireland (−21.2%) is the most striking drop in the top 10. Ireland consistently ranks high because many AWS and Fastly edge nodes serving European traffic are registered there. The decline from 159M to 125M suggests a shift in CDN routing or a reduction in specific large-scale automation jobs that previously routed through Dublin data centres. This is a CDN-topology story more than a developer story.

Hong Kong (+65.5%) posted the strongest growth in the top 20, moving from 16th to 15th and nearly doubling its February volume. This is consistent with growing adoption of Ruby in Southeast Asian fintech and SaaS startups, many of which use Hong Kong as a regional cloud hub.

Brazil (+37.7%) and Russia (+32.9%) both posted strong gains, suggesting expanding local Ruby communities and increased CI automation in those markets. South Korea (−17.2%) bucked the trend, sliding back from a strong February — likely a normalisation after an unusually high-volume month rather than a structural retreat.

CDN note: Download counts are attributed to the geographic location of the requesting IP address. Cloud provider infrastructure (especially AWS us-east-1 via Ireland and US CDN nodes) means country-level figures reflect both end-developer activity and automated CI/CD pipeline traffic.

bundler continues to sit far ahead of all other gems with over 78 million downloads — nearly three times the next entry. Its dominance reflects that almost every Ruby project, CI job, and deployment pipeline resolves and installs dependencies through Bundler.

Stdlib backport gems — base64, bigdecimal, logger — continue to rank high as the Ruby community migrates projects across Ruby 3.x minor versions, where several previously built-in libraries were moved to bundled gems requiring explicit declaration.

🤖 mcp — The AI Protocol Gem Explodes (+814.9%)

mcp is the standout story of March 2026. The gem provides a Ruby implementation of the Model Context Protocol (MCP) — an open standard introduced by Anthropic for connecting AI agents and LLMs to external data sources and tools. With the broader Ruby and Rails community rapidly integrating LLM-based features into applications, demand for a first-class MCP client/server library has spiked dramatically. Downloads jumped from 348K in February to over 3.1 million in March — a nearly 9× increase. Expect this trajectory to continue as more teams prototype agentic Rails features.

📡 agora_dynamic_key — Real-Time Communication Gains (+356.3%)

agora_dynamic_key provides token generation for the Agora real-time audio/video communication platform. The sharp rise points to a growing number of Ruby backends powering real-time features — live streaming, voice channels, and in-app video — particularly in Asian SaaS products. This aligns with the surge in downloads from Hong Kong and Singapore noted in the geographic section.

🪟 win32-registry — Windows Ruby Seeing Renewed Activity (+322.0%)

win32-registry exposes the Windows Registry API to Ruby. A sudden spike is unusual and typically points to a tooling update in a widely-used Windows-centric project — such as a DevKit, RubyInstaller component, or enterprise automation suite — that pulled in this dependency at scale. Worth monitoring in April to see whether it normalises or reflects a sustained shift in Windows-based Ruby tooling usage.

🔬 app_profiler — Shopify's Profiler Goes Wide (+206.6%)

app_profiler is Shopify's open-source application profiler gem, designed to sample Rack-based Ruby applications in production. A 2× growth suggests that performance engineering practices pioneered at Shopify scale are spreading to mid-size Rails shops. Combined with the rise of crystalball (+125.5%), a predictive test selection gem, March shows a clear uptick in Ruby teams investing in developer productivity and observability tooling.

🔍 rubocop-minitest — Broad Linting Adoption (+143.7%)

rubocop-minitest adds Minitest-specific cops to RuboCop. It entered March with an already-healthy 634K downloads and ended with 1.5M — the largest absolute increase among emerging gems. This scale of growth typically indicates a coordinated rollout: major open-source organisations, agencies, or large monorepos enabling the plugin in their shared RuboCop config and propagating it across hundreds of CI jobs. It also reflects the strong showing of minitest itself in the top gems list (#9 with 23M downloads).

🐘 clickhouse-activerecord — Analytics in the Rails Stack (+141.1%)

clickhouse-activerecord provides an ActiveRecord adapter for ClickHouse, enabling Rails models to connect directly to ClickHouse databases. Its 141% growth reflects the expanding use of ClickHouse as an analytical backend within Rails applications — teams that previously exported data to a separate analytics system are increasingly querying ClickHouse directly from their Rails app layer. This is one to watch as ClickHouse adoption in the Ruby ecosystem continues.

🏢 activerecord-multi-tenant — SaaS Multi-Tenancy (+148.1%)

activerecord-multi-tenant simplifies row-level multi-tenancy in ActiveRecord by scoping queries automatically. Its growth aligns with the continued boom in B2B SaaS built on Rails, where multi-tenancy is a foundational architectural requirement. The gem's adoption is rising broadly rather than being driven by a single large project.

Key Takeaways

March 2026 tells a consistent story: the Ruby ecosystem is healthy, growing in breadth, and actively absorbing new paradigms. Three themes stand out:

  • AI integration is here. The mcp gem's explosion is not a fluke — it is the leading indicator of LLM tooling becoming mainstream in the Rails world.
  • Observability and quality tooling is expanding. app_profiler, crystalball, and rubocop-minitest all surged simultaneously, suggesting engineering teams are investing heavily in the inner loop.
  • Geographic growth is diversifying. Hong Kong, Brazil, and the Netherlands all posted strong gains, pointing to a Ruby developer base that is becoming less concentrated in the US and Western Europe.

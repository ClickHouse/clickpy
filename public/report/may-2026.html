PyPI Downloads: Monthly Insights Report

May 2026  ·  Data source: PyPI download logs via ClickHouse  ·  Powered by ClickPy

PyPI recorded 163.8 billion total downloads in May 2026, up from 150.1 billion in April — a month-over-month increase of +9.2%. Downloads have grown +19.8% over three months (from 136.7 billion in March), pointing to sustained, broad ecosystem expansion. The most striking signal this month is the accelerating dominance of AI/ML-adjacent packages, with LLM orchestration and agent frameworks collectively accounting for some of the highest growth rates ever observed at meaningful download volumes.

Commentary

The United States continues to dominate, accounting for 74.8% of all global PyPI downloads — a share that has remained remarkably stable across recent months. At 122.5 billion downloads, US volume alone dwarfs the rest of the world combined. This is consistent with the concentration of large-scale cloud CI/CD infrastructure and hyperscaler mirror traffic in the US.

The United Kingdom surged +26.1% MoM, jumping from 4.1 billion in April to 5.2 billion in May. This is a notable acceleration for a historically stable market, pushing the UK into a statistical tie with Germany for the #2 spot globally. The growth appears distributed across infrastructure and AI tooling packages rather than a single project, suggesting genuine broadening of Python adoption in UK-based cloud environments.

India posted the second-largest percentage gain among top-10 countries at +26.2%, reaching 2.5 billion downloads. India's rise is consistent with a multi-month trend — downloads have roughly doubled since late 2025. The top packages in India mirror the global list closely (boto3, packaging, certifi, requests), indicating that CI pipeline automation and cloud-native workloads are driving the growth rather than a narrow niche.

China accelerated to +14.8% MoM, crossing the 1.1 billion mark. Given the historically conservative growth rate from this region, the uptick is worth monitoring. Spain was a notable outlier to the upside at +21.6%, moving from 375 million to 456 million downloads.

Brazil bucked the trend with a -15.0% decline, dropping from 1.0 billion to 860 million downloads — the most significant month-over-month contraction among the top-20 countries. Brazil had shown consistent growth in earlier months, so this dip is potentially transient, but it is worth watching in June. Singapore also dipped -4.0%, a mild contraction for a key APAC infrastructure hub.

litellm is on a multi-month tear — from 97.7 million in March to 513.4 million in May, a +425.6% three-month surge. LiteLLM functions as a universal proxy/translation layer across LLM providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, etc.), and its growth is directly correlated with teams wanting provider-agnostic inference without rewriting prompt code.

pydantic-ai-slim saw downloads climb from 144.9 million to 268.3 million (+85.1%). Its tight integration with the pydantic ecosystem gives it a structural advantage — teams adding agent capabilities to existing FastAPI or data-validation workflows can adopt it without introducing an alien dependency graph.

langchain-protocol exploded +524.7% MoM, from 6.0 million to 37.4 million, signalling that the LangChain ecosystem's modular protocol layer is being widely adopted as teams standardise inter-agent communication interfaces. google-adk (Google's Agent Development Kit) grew +193.9% MoM and +253.9% over three months, gaining traction as the canonical SDK for agents interoperating with Google Cloud services and Gemini models.

browser-use grew from 10.2 million to 23.6 million (+132.1% MoM). This package, which enables LLM-driven browser automation, represents a fast-growing niche: AI agents that interact with the web as a human would, rather than calling structured APIs. strands-agents (AWS's open-source agent framework) posted +80.5% MoM; its AWS provenance makes its longer-term trajectory one to watch.

⚡ Theme 2: LLM Inference Infrastructure

sglang is the standout story of the three-month window: downloads grew from 35.9 million in March to 228 million in April to 303.9 million in May — a +744% three-month surge. SGLang (Structured Generation Language) is a high-throughput LLM serving framework developed at UC Berkeley/LMSYS. Its growth is driven by ML platform teams deploying open-weight models (LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen) who need a production-grade alternative to vLLM. At 303 million monthly downloads, it is no longer a research curiosity.

🖥️ Theme 3: NVIDIA CUDA 13 Ecosystem Rollout

A cluster of NVIDIA packages — including nvidia-cublas, nvidia-cudnn-cu13, nvidia-nccl-cu13, and related cu13 variants — each grew +300–330% over three months (from ~8 million in March to ~33–36 million in May). The synchronised growth across the entire CUDA 13 package family is characteristic of a major PyTorch CUDA wheel release cycle: when PyTorch ships wheels that depend on a new CUDA toolkit version, all associated NVIDIA library packages get pulled as dependencies en masse.

🌳 Theme 4: Code Intelligence Tooling

A family of tree-sitter-* grammar packages — including tree-sitter-bash, tree-sitter-rust, tree-sitter-c, tree-sitter-go, and tree-sitter-java — all grew between +238% and +255% over three months. The breadth of language grammars seeing concurrent growth points to platform-level adoption in AI code intelligence products that need to parse and understand source code structure — rather than isolated developer tooling.

🗄️ Theme 5: Data Infrastructure and Authentication

ydb (Yandex Database Python SDK) grew +96.1% MoM and +337.8% over three months, reaching 155.6 million downloads. joserfc more than doubled (+104.1%) to 112 million downloads — its growth tracks the general expansion of OAuth2/OIDC adoption in Python web services, particularly as agent frameworks increasingly require token-based auth flows. databricks-sql-connector reached 163.4 million downloads (+70.3%), reflecting continued growth of Databricks as a Python-first data platform. backports-zstd nearly doubled (+96.7%) to 68.6 million, driven by packages targeting Python 3.11–3.13 that want Zstandard compression without waiting for the 3.14 stdlib.

4. Key Takeaways for Python Developers

  1. AI/agent frameworks are no longer "experimental" dependencies. Packages like litellm, pydantic-ai-slim, and crewai are being downloaded hundreds of millions of times per month — volumes that suggest production deployments, not just prototyping.
  2. The LLM inference tier is maturing. sglang's +744% three-month growth signals that teams running their own open-weight model inference are converging on purpose-built serving stacks rather than general-purpose web servers.
  3. Tree-sitter is becoming infrastructure. Its grammar packages are growing in lockstep across multiple languages, indicating that code-aware AI tooling is being deployed at platform scale.
  4. India and the UK are the most dynamic growth markets among the top-10 countries, both posting +26% MoM. Developers and teams in these regions are clearly ramping up Python-based cloud and AI workloads faster than the global average.
  5. The core dependency stack is not going anywhere. boto3, requests, packaging, and urllib3 sit at 1.6–3.3 billion monthly downloads each. Python's long tail of transitive dependencies continues to be the invisible connective tissue of the global software supply chain.

PyPI Usage Report: September 2025

Monthly Insights for Python Developers

Executive Summary

September 2025 saw 83.6 billion downloads from PyPI, representing a 4.9% increase over August's 79.7 billion downloads. This growth reflects continued expansion in Python adoption, particularly driven by AI/ML infrastructure and cloud computing workloads. The geographic distribution remained stable with the United States dominating, while emerging packages showed strong momentum in AI tooling and development workflow automation.

Trends & Insights

AI Infrastructure Boom: Packages like fastmcp, genai-prices, and open-webui show triple-digit growth, indicating AI development has moved from experimentation to production deployment.

Rust-powered performance: Both fastuuid and prek leverage Rust for performance-critical tasks, reflecting a broader trend of using Rust bindings to accelerate Python tooling.

Cloud-native patterns: Strong growth in async cloud clients and data infrastructure demonstrates Python's evolution toward cloud-native, event-driven architectures.

Conclusion

September 2025 demonstrated healthy growth across the Python ecosystem, with total downloads increasing 4.9% to reach 83.6 billion. While established infrastructure packages like boto3 continue to dominate, the explosive growth in AI tooling and Rust-enhanced utilities signals important shifts in how Python developers are building and deploying applications.

The geographic distribution remained stable, though Singapore's 30% growth suggests accelerating cloud infrastructure deployment in Asia-Pacific. For developers planning technology investments, emerging trends clearly point toward: (1) AI/LLM application infrastructure, (2) performance optimization through Rust bindings, and (3) enhanced developer productivity tooling.
